Initial Approach & Methodology


The original requirement was to make something that gives advice to people from multiple perspectives. My teammates and I came up with a simple list of user needs and approaches to satisfy them. These included:

  • A user should be able to enter a prompt
  • User privacy
  • User history should be kept
  • A system that generates three perspectives for a provided prompt
  • A personalized system that learns from the user's preferences

At this stage, our persective scale ranged from optimistic to neutral to pessimistic. We mapped out the requirements and matched them to the desired functionalities and any potential technological tools required.

Iterations


Our framework initially relied on the OpenAI API to generate all outputs based on a single prompt. However, we quickly realized that we needed tighter and more specific constraints to achieve optimal results. Over the course of two months, we implemented the following changes:

  • Narrowing Down the User Scope
    Targeting a broad audience proved too unfocused and did not guarantee accurate, relevant outcomes. Therefore, we decided to focus on business leaders and managers, who frequently face complex, high-stakes decisions.

  • Refining Perspective Generation
    Our initial approach used optimistic, neutral, and pessimistic viewpoints, which became less relevant for our new, more specialized user group. As a result, we removed that framework and prompted the LLM to generate three perspectives tailored to each specific input.

  • Restricting the LLM
    Since users could enter a wide variety of prompts, we introduced filters to block input unrelated to business advice and decision-making, ensuring more focused and useful output.

  • Comparison Effect
    Comparing diverse perspectives is essential for effective decision-making. We added a side-by-side view to let users easily compare and contrast different insights. Additionally, we included a SWOT analysis for each generated perspective to facilitate more straightforward evaluation.

  • Action and Tracking
    Some recommendations require implementation over time with multiple steps. To address this, we created a dedicated page for any chosen perspective, allowing users to follow actionable steps and track their progress.
